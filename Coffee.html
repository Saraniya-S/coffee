Our story

A counter, a kettle, and no rush

"We measured our first year in pours, not sales."

Amber Hour started as a single filter cone on a borrowed counter. We kept the ritual small on purpose — a short menu, beans roasted in batches we can taste-test by hand, and a bar built for conversation rather than speed.

Every seat faces the counter. You can watch your coffee get made, ask the barista what's brewing, or just sit with the smell of it for a while. That's the whole idea.

The signature pour

The Ritual

Every pour-over on our bar follows the same four-stage timing, refined over six years of tasting notes. It's not decoration — it's the actual recipe our baristas time on the bar clock.

Same beans, same water temperature, same 2:30 window, every single time. Consistency is the whole craft.

"The bloom is where the coffee tells you what it's going to taste like."
0:00 — 0:30
Bloom
40g of just-off-boil water, enough to saturate the grounds. We let it sit and release CO₂ before pouring further.
0:30 — 1:00
First pour
Slow concentric circles to 200g, keeping the water level steady just below the rim of the filter.
1:00 — 1:45
Second pour
Up to 350g total, slightly faster, to keep the bed agitated and the extraction even.
1:45 — 2:30
Draw-down
The last of the water filters through. A clean draw-down means a clean cup — no bitterness, no mud.
